## Chart Selection Justification

## 1. Sales Trend View

### Business Question

How are sales changing over time?

### Why this Chart?

A **line chart** is the most appropriate visualization for displaying sales trends because it clearly illustrates increases, decreases, and seasonal fluctuations across different time periods.

### Fields Used

* **Columns:** Order Date (Month/Year)
* **Rows:** Sales
* **Color:** Sales
* **Filter:** Region, Category, Customer Segment

### Design Principle Applied

* Chronological order was maintained.
* Minimal clutter with a single trend line.
* Easy identification of sales peaks and declines.

### Mistake Avoided

A bar chart was avoided because it is less effective than a line chart for showing continuous trends over time.

---

## 2. Regional Performance View

### Business Question

Which region contributes the highest sales?

### Why this Chart?

A **horizontal bar chart** allows easy comparison of sales across multiple regions while making differences immediately visible.

### Fields Used

* **Rows:** Region
* **Columns:** Sales
* **Color:** Profit
* **Label:** Sales

### Design Principle Applied

* Regions are sorted by performance.
* Color is used to provide additional context without creating visual clutter.
* Labels improve readability.

### Mistake Avoided

A pie chart was avoided because comparing regional sales values is easier using bars than slice sizes.

---

## 3. Category Profitability View

### Business Question

Which product categories and sub-categories generate the highest profit?

### Why this Chart?

A **horizontal bar chart** clearly ranks categories and sub-categories based on profitability, allowing management to quickly identify the strongest profit contributors.

### Fields Used

* **Rows:** Category and Sub-Category
* **Columns:** Profit
* **Color:** Profit
* **Label:** Profit

### Design Principle Applied

* Categories are ranked from highest to lowest profit.
* Consistent color encoding improves interpretation.
* Profit labels provide precise values.

### Mistake Avoided

Treemaps were avoided because they make precise profit comparisons more difficult than bar charts.

---

## 4. Customer Segment View

### Business Question

Which customer segment contributes the most sales?

### Why this Chart?

A **bar chart** effectively compares sales generated by each customer segment.

### Fields Used

* **Rows:** Customer Segment
* **Columns:** Sales
* **Color:** Profit
* **Label:** Sales

### Design Principle Applied

* Segments are displayed clearly for quick comparison.
* Labels improve readability.
* Profit color adds additional business context.

### Mistake Avoided

Stacked charts were avoided because they make comparing segment totals more difficult.

---

## 5. Discount vs Profit View

### Business Question

How does discount influence profitability?

### Why this Chart?

A **scatter plot** is the most appropriate visualization for identifying relationships between two numerical variables.

### Fields Used

* **Columns:** Discount
* **Rows:** Profit
* **Detail:** Individual Orders
* **Color:** Category

### Design Principle Applied

* Each point represents an individual order.
* Scatter distribution makes the relationship between discount and profit immediately visible.
* Color distinguishes different product categories.

### Mistake Avoided

Bar charts were avoided because they cannot effectively display relationships between two continuous variables.

---

## 6. Shipping Performance View

### Business Question

How do shipping modes compare in terms of delivery performance?

### Why this Chart?

A **bar chart** allows quick comparison of average delivery days across different shipping modes.

### Fields Used

* **Rows:** Ship Mode
* **Columns:** Average Delivery Days
* **Color:** Shipping Delay Bucket
* **Label:** Average Delivery Days

### Design Principle Applied

* Clear comparison between shipping methods.
* Color highlights delivery performance categories.
* Simple layout minimizes unnecessary visual elements.

### Mistake Avoided

Tables were avoided because visual comparisons are significantly faster using bar charts.

---

# Overall Dashboard Design Principles

The dashboard was designed following executive reporting best practices:

* Appropriate chart types were selected based on the business question.
* Consistent colors and formatting were applied throughout the dashboard.
* KPI cards summarize key business metrics.
* Interactive filters enable users to analyze results by Region, Category, Customer Segment, Date, and Ship Mode.
* Charts are arranged to support a logical business narrative, beginning with overall performance and progressing toward operational analysis.
* Unnecessary decorative elements and visual clutter were intentionally avoided to improve readability and decision-making.
